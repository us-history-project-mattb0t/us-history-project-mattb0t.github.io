Japan invades Manchuria

September 18, 1931

Japan invades Manchuria over the Mukden Incident, where Japanese soldiers dressed like the Chinese and attacked their own train station near Mukden. Following this fabrication, the Japanese government declares war upon Chinese government (the Kuomintang, headed by Generalissimo Chiang Kai Shek).

The Japanese fabricated the Mukden incident in order to obtain the valuable natural resources in Manchuria that they desperately needed. The Japanese, once they controlled Manchuria, created a puppet government called Manchukuo. It is the start of the Japanese, imperialist invasion of China.

Hitler and Stalin invade Poland

September 1, 1939

Hitler's Nazi Germany and Stalin's Soviet Union begin their joint invasion of Poland. The Allies are sending very limited aid to the defense of Poland. Most Poles have lost hope, and are ordering a mass evacuation of soldiers to Romania.

Poland will be divided into two regions upon capitulation: one that will be occupied by Nazi Germany, and the other that will be occupied by the Soviet Union. Before the invasion, the two nations signed a treaty: the Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact. This treaty was a pact of non-aggression, meaning that they promised not to attack each other and invade Poland together.
